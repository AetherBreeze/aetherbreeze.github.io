The One Major Problem

I‚Äôll be honest with you: we‚Äôve reached the one major problem with this method of deriving quantum mechanics. However, we are not doomed. We can still derive the momentum operator in a vaguely rigorous fashion by stealing an idea from Einstein: the unification of energy and momentum.

The (very tenuous) idea goes like this. In special relativity, energy and momentum are unified into a single vector. Special relativity essentially treats energy as time momentum. The only differences between the two are a factor of the speed of light ccc, which we will unjustifiably ignore, and a sign flip.
